Methodos and Research Process

Phase 1: Literature Review & Hypothesis Formulation

Conducted an extensive review of existing academic articles from the last five years, focusing on developmental psychology and media studies. This phase led to the creation of a refined, testable hypothesis focused on the type of social media engagement.

Phase 2: Data Collection & Survey Deployment

Designed a four-part anonymous survey approved by the Ethics Committee, utilizing the GAD-7 scale for anxiety measurement. Successfully gathered data from the target sample, ensuring demographic diversity to strengthen external validity.

Phase 3: Analysis & Findings

Used **SPSS** to perform correlational analysis and regression modeling. The data supported the hypothesis, showing a strong positive correlation ($r = 0.58$) between time spent passively viewing others' posts and higher anxiety scores, but a weak correlation with active communication.
